# CINEMATOGRAPHER'S VISUAL LANGUAGE REFERENCE

## SHOT GRAMMAR

### Shot Size Psychology

| Shot | Abbreviation | Frame | Emotional Function | Use When |
|------|--------------|-------|-------------------|----------|
| **Extreme Close-Up** | ECU | Single feature (eye, lips, object) | Intimacy, intensity, revelation | Emotional climax, detail reveal |
| **Close-Up** | CU | Face fills frame | Emotion, connection, reaction | Character moments, emotional beats |
| **Medium Close-Up** | MCU | Head and shoulders | Conversational, presentational | Dialogue, explanation, host |
| **Medium Shot** | MS | Waist up | Context with intimacy | Interaction, demonstration |
| **Medium Long Shot** | MLS | Knees up | Body language visible | Movement, gesture |
| **Long Shot** | LS | Full body with environment | Context, scale, isolation | Establishing, transition |
| **Extreme Long Shot** | ELS | Subject small in vast environment | Epic scale, insignificance | Opening, location establish |

### Camera Angle Psychology

| Angle | Emotional Effect | Best For |
|-------|-----------------|----------|
| **Eye Level** | Neutral, equal, relatable | Standard coverage, documentary |
| **Low Angle** | Power, dominance, aspiration | Heroes, products, architecture |
| **High Angle** | Vulnerability, overview, observation | Establishing, emotional weakness |
| **Dutch/Canted** | Unease, tension, disorientation | Thriller, horror, instability |
| **Bird's Eye** | Omniscience, pattern, abstraction | Montage, choreography |
| **Worm's Eye** | Extreme drama, monumentality | Product hero, architecture |

## LENS PSYCHOLOGY

### Focal Length Emotional Language

| Focal Length | Visual Effect | Emotional Quality | Best For |
|--------------|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| **14-24mm** | Extreme wide, distortion | Disorientation, vastness, intimacy (close) | Architecture, horror, immersive |
| **24-35mm** | Wide, environmental | Context-rich, journalistic | Documentary, environmental portrait |
| **35-50mm** | "Normal," cinematic | Neutral, filmic, balanced | Narrative cinema standard |

| **85-135mm** | Flattering compression | Beauty, isolation | Portrait, fashion, product |

| Aperture | DOF | Emotional Effect | Use Case |
|----------|-----|-----------------|----------|
| **T1.4-2** | Razor thin | Intense focus, dreamlike | Romantic, luxury, isolation |
| **T2.8-4** | Shallow | Cinematic separation | Standard narrative |
| **T5.6-8** | Moderate | Environmental context | Documentary, wide shots |
| **T11+** | Deep | Everything matters equally | Landscape, architecture, Wes Anderson |

## CAMERA MOVEMENT VOCABULARY

### Movement Types and Purpose

| Movement | Execution | Emotional Purpose | Avoid When |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|------------|
| **Push In** | Dolly/gimbal forward | Engagement, revelation, intimacy | Subject retreating emotionally |
| **Pull Out** | Dolly/gimbal backward | Revelation, isolation, loss | Building intimacy |
| **Track/Dolly** | Lateral movement | Following, observation, journey | Static subject |
| **Orbit** | 360° around subject | Examination, power, transformation | Quick scenes |
| **Crane Up** | Vertical rising | Triumph, revelation, escape | Grounding moments |
| **Crane Down** | Vertical descending | Arrival, grounding, focus | Aspirational moments |
| **Handheld** | Organic shake | Urgency, authenticity, chaos | Luxury, stability |
| **Steadicam/Gimbal** | Smooth float | Grace, observation, dreamlike | Gritty realism |
| **Static/Locked** | Tripod, no movement | Contemplation, respect, formality | High energy |
| **Whip Pan** | Rapid horizontal | Energy, transition, surprise | Contemplative |
| **Rack Focus** | Focus shift | Attention redirect, connection | When subjects equal importance |

### Movement Speed as Emotion

| Speed | Character | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Imperceptible** (1-2cm/s) | Breathing with subject | Luxury, contemplative |
| **Slow** (5-10cm/s) | Deliberate examination | Product, documentary |
| **Medium** (15-25cm/s) | Following action | Narrative standard |
| **Fast** (50cm+/s) | Urgency, chaos | Action, transition |

| **Hard** | Directional, sharp shadows | Drama, tension, definition | Noir, thriller, masculine |
| **Soft** | Wrapped, diffused | Romance, beauty, comfort | Beauty, lifestyle, feminine |

| **Under** | Unnatural, horror | Monster, villain, unease |

## EDITING RHYTHM

### Cut Timing

| Pace | Avg Shot Length | Creates | Genre |
|------|-----------------|---------|-------|
| **Slow** | 8-15s | Contemplation, tension | Drama, horror, art |
| **Moderate** | 4-8s | Engagement, story | Standard narrative |
| **Fast** | 1-3s | Energy, chaos, montage | Action, trailer, commercial |
| **Rapid** | <1s | Overwhelm, subliminal | Music video, extreme action |

### Transition Types

| Transition | Effect | Use When |
|------------|--------|----------|
| **Hard Cut** | Immediate, jarring | Standard, impact |
| **Dissolve** | Time passage, connection | Passage, relation |
| **Fade** | Beginning/end, separation | Major shift, conclusion |
| **Wipe** | Stylized, retro | Specific style choice |
| **Match Cut** | Visual/conceptual rhyme | Poetry, connection |
| **J-Cut** | Audio leads video | Anticipation |
| **L-Cut** | Audio trails video | Continuation |

## PRODUCTION VALUE INDICATORS

Elements that scream "high production":

1. **Motivated lighting** — Light comes from logical sources
2. **Negative fill** — Controlled shadows, not just less light
3. **Depth** — Foreground, midground, background elements
4. **Atmosphere** — Haze, dust, volumetric light
5. **Movement** — Camera motion with purpose
6. **Focus** — Intentional depth of field choices
7. **Color** — Graded, unified, purposeful
8. **Sound** — Designed, not just recorded
9. **Imperfection** — Lens flares, grain, breathing
10. **Detail** — Set dressing, wardrobe, props that tell story
